# Top 20 Mathematical Models in Marketing Papers

Based on a comprehensive search of Google Scholar and academic resources, here are 20 influential mathematical models and their key papers.

## 1. Bass Diffusion Model
**Key Paper:** Bass, Frank M. (1969), "A New Product Growth Model for Consumer Durables." *Management Science*.
**Description:** Predicts the adoption of new products using coefficients of innovation (p) and imitation (q).

## 2. Customer Lifetime Value (CLV)
**Key Paper:** Berger, Paul D., and Nasr, N.I. (1998), "Customer Lifetime Value: Marketing Models and Applications." *Journal of Interactive Marketing*.
**Description:** Calculates the net present value of future profits from a customer.

## 3. Marketing Mix Modeling (MMM)
**Key Paper:** Little, John D. C. (1979), "Aggregate Advertising Response Models: The State of the Art."
**Description:** Quantifies the impact of 4Ps on sales.

## 4. Discrete Choice Models (Logit)
**Key Paper:** McFadden, Daniel (1974), "Conditional Logit Analysis of Qualitative Choice Behavior."
**Description:** Models consumer choices among alternatives.

## 5. Brand Equity (Keller's Model)
**Key Paper:** Keller, Kevin Lane (1993), "Conceptualizing, Measuring, and Managing Customer-Based Brand Equity."
**Description:** Framework for measuring brand value.

## 6. Advertising Response (Vidale-Wolfe)
**Key Paper:** Vidale, M. L., and Wolfe, H. B. (1957), "An Operations-Research Study of Sales Response to Advertising."
**Description:** Models sales response to ad spend with decay.

## 7. Dirichlet Model of Buying Behavior
**Key Paper:** Ehrenberg, A. S. C. (1959), "The Pattern of Consumer Purchases."
**Description:** Stochastic model for purchase frequency and brand choice.

## 8. Game Theory in Marketing
**Key Paper:** Moorthy, K. Sridhar (1985), "Using Game Theory to Model Competition."
**Description:** Strategic interactions between firms.

## 9. Conjoint Analysis
**Key Paper:** Green, Paul E., and Rao, Vithala R. (1971), "Conjoint Measurement for Quantifying Judgmental Data."
**Description:** Measures how consumers value different attributes.

## 10. Service Quality (SERVQUAL)
**Key Paper:** Parasuraman, A., Zeithaml, V. A., & Berry, L. L. (1988), "SERVQUAL: A multiple-item scale for measuring consumer perc."
**Description:** Measures gap between customer expectations and perceptions.

## 11. Relationship Marketing (KMV Model)
**Key Paper:** Morgan, R. M., & Hunt, S. D. (1994), "The Commitment-Trust Theory of Relationship Marketing."
**Description:** Models trust and commitment as key drivers.

## 12. Market Segmentation
**Key Paper:** Wedel, M., & Kamakura, W. A. (2000), "Market Segmentation: Conceptual and Methodological Foundations."
**Description:** Mathematical approaches to grouping consumers.

## 13. Price Elasticity Models
**Key Paper:** Tellis, G. J. (1988), "The Price Elasticity of Selective Demand."
**Description:** Meta-analysis of price elasticity.

## 14. Satisfaction-Loyalty Link
**Key Paper:** Oliver, Richard L. (1980), "A Cognitive Model of the Antecedents and Consequences of Satisfaction Decisions."
**Description:** Expectancy-disconfirmation model.

## 15. Viral Marketing (Epidemiological Models)
**Key Paper:** Leskovec, J., Adamic, L. A., & Huberman, B. A. (2007), "The Dynamics of Viral Marketing."
**Description:** Applies SIR/SEIR models to information spread.

## 16. Bayesian Inference in Marketing
**Key Paper:** Rossi, P. E., & Allenby, G. M. (2003), "Bayesian Statistics and Marketing."
**Description:** Hierarchical models for individual heterogeneity.

## 17. Churn Prediction Models
**Key Paper:** Neslin, S. A., et al. (2006), "Defection Detection: Measuring and Understanding the Predictive Accuracy of Customer Churn Models."
**Description:** Logistic regression and decision trees for churn.

## 18. Recommendation Systems (Collaborative Filtering)
**Key Paper:** Adomavicius, G., & Tuzhilin, A. (2005), "Toward the Next Generation of Recommender Systems."
**Description:** Matrix factorization and neighbor-based methods.

## 19. Social Network Analysis
**Key Paper:** Goldenberg, J., Han, S., Lehmann, D. R., & Hong, J. W. (2009), "The Role of Hubs in the Adoption Process."
**Description:** Graph theory applied to influence.

## 20. Attribution Modeling
**Key Paper:** Kannan, P. K., et al. (2016), "Path to Purchase: A Framework for Attribution Modeling."
**Description:** Assigning credit to touchpoints.
