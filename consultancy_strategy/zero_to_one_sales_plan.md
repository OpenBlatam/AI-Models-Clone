# Zero-to-One: Action Plan to Get Your First Consultancy Sale

**Current State:** No sales yet.
**Goal:** Secure the first paying client.

Based on the research (Intrinsic Selling + Trust Building), here is your immediate action plan.

## Phase 1: Define & Package (Days 1-3)

### 1. Define Your "Micro-Niche"
Stop being a "Business Consultant." Be a "Specific Problem Solver."
- *Bad:* "I help companies grow."
- *Good:* "I help small marketing agencies automate their reporting using AI."
- **Action:** Write down clearly: "I help [WHO] solve [PROBLEM] so they can [RESULT]."

### 2. Create a "Foot-in-the-Door" Offer
It is hard to sell a $10k contract to a stranger. It is easy to sell a $500 audit or strategy session.
- **Create a "Roadmap" or "Audit" product.**
    - *Price:* Low (or free for the first 3 people).
    - *Deliverable:* A document/plan they can keep.
    - *Goal:* Demonstrate **Ability** and **Benevolence** (Trust Equation).

## Phase 2: Build Proof (Days 4-7)

### 3. The "Mock" Case Study
Since you have no past clients:
- Pick a target company (or a typical one).
- Write a detailed article: "How I would solve [Company X]'s [Big Problem]."
- Outline the strategy, the math, and the expected results.
- **Publish this** on LinkedIn/Blog. This IS your portfolio.

### 4. The "Beta" Client
- Find 3 people in your network (or extend to 2nd degree).
- **The Pitch:** "I’m launching a new service specializing in [Niche]. I’m looking for 3 beta testers to get the full service for free (or 80% off) in exchange for honest feedback and a testimonial/case study."
- **Goal:** You need **Performance Proof** more than you need money right now.

## Phase 3: Outbound & Content (Ongoing)

### 5. "Intrinsic" Selling Conversations
Don't pitch. Diagnose.
- When talking to potential leads, ask detailed questions about their pain.
- Give away the "What" (the strategy) for free. Sell the "How" (the implementation).
- If they leave the meeting thinking "Wow, that person really understands my problem," you have sold them on YOU.

### 6. Daily Knowledge Sharing
- Post one insight daily related to your niche.
- Don't just post news. Post **opinions** and **advice**.
- *Format:* "Most companies do X. Here is why you should do Y."

## Immediate Checklist
- [ ] Define your Niche Statement.
- [ ] Create a 1-page PDF describing your "Foot-in-the-Door" offer.
- [ ] Identify 10 people to ask for a "Beta" test.
- [ ] Write one "Mock Case Study" article.

## Recommended Methodologies for execution

### The "SPIN" Model (for Sales Calls)
- **S**ituation: Ask about their current state.
- **P**roblem: Ask about the difficulties they face.
- **I**mplication: Ask what happens if they *don't* solve it (pain).
- **N**eed-Payoff: Ask how valuable a solution would be.
*Reference: Neil Rackham, "SPIN Selling"*

### The "Challenger" Approach (for Content)
- Don't just agree with the client.
- Teach them something new about their business.
- Tailor your message to their specific resonance points.
- Take control of the conversation.
*Reference: Dixon & Adamson, "The Challenger Sale"*
